LANGUAGE & STYLE:
Target language is Swedish. Use formal "ni" for UI, "du" for casual lore.
Swedish is Germanic with relatively simple conjugation and noun gender.

TERMS:
Common Swedish words: och, att, det, som, en, ett, i, på, är, för, med, till, av, den, de, inte, har, kan, ska, kommer, blir, vara, från, men, eller, när, då, där, hur

GRAMMAR:
Swedish uses Latin alphabet with additional letters: å, ä, ö.
Two grammatical genders: common (en) and neuter (ett).
Definiteness is marked by suffixes: en bok (a book) → boken (the book).
Adjectives agree with noun gender and number: en stor bok, ett stort hus, stora böcker.
Verbs do not conjugate for person — only tense: är (is), var (was), har (has), hade (had).
Word order: V2 in main clauses (verb always second position).
