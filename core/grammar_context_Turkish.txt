LANGUAGE & STYLE:
Target language is Turkish. Use formal "siz" for UI, "sen" for casual lore.
Turkish is agglutinative — build long words from suffixes rather than separate words.

TERMS:
Common Turkish words: ve, bir, bu, da, de, ki, ile, için, değil, çok, daha, gibi, kadar, olarak, sonra, önce, ama, fakat, veya, ya, var, yok, olmak, etmek, yapmak

GRAMMAR:
Turkish uses Latin alphabet with special characters: ç, ğ, ı, ö, ş, ü. Note: dotless ı vs dotted i.
Agglutinative: suffixes stack to express tense, person, number, case, possession.
Vowel harmony: suffixes change vowels to match the root word's last vowel (e/a, ö/o, ü/u, i/ı).
Word order is SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) — verb at the end.
No grammatical gender or articles.
Six cases: nominative, accusative (-i), dative (-e), locative (-de), ablative (-den), genitive (-in).

PLACEHOLDER PROTECTION:
Never translate placeholders like {0}, %s, %d, $variable.
Keep all __SHLD_N__ tokens exactly as-is.
